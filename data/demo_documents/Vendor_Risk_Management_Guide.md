# Vendor Risk Management Guide

Company: HarbourBridge Digital Finance Limited
Owner: Procurement and Risk
Version: 2026.1

## Purpose

This guide explains how HarbourBridge evaluates and monitors vendors that support technology, operations, compliance, customer support, and data processing activities.

## Vendor Tiers

Tier 1 vendors support critical services, process restricted data, connect to production systems, or could materially affect customers if unavailable. Tier 2 vendors support important internal processes or handle confidential data. Tier 3 vendors have limited operational impact and do not access confidential or restricted data.

## Required Due Diligence

Vendor due diligence may include:

- Business owner risk assessment.
- Information security questionnaire.
- Data protection review.
- Business continuity and disaster recovery evidence.
- Financial stability review for critical vendors.
- Compliance review for regulated or outsourced activities.
- Contract review for confidentiality, audit rights, breach notification, and subcontractor controls.

## Risks Requiring Escalation

The following vendor risks require escalation to Risk and Compliance:

- Vendor will process customer personal data or restricted data outside approved locations.
- Vendor lacks breach notification obligations in the contract.
- Vendor cannot provide evidence of access control, encryption, or vulnerability management.
- Vendor uses subcontractors without disclosure or approval rights.
- Vendor supports a critical service but lacks tested business continuity plans.
- Vendor has adverse media involving fraud, sanctions, regulatory penalties, or major security incidents.
- Vendor requests production access without a named business owner and approved access scope.

## Ongoing Monitoring

Tier 1 vendors are reviewed annually. Tier 2 vendors are reviewed every two years. Trigger events such as security incidents, ownership changes, service outages, or material scope changes require off-cycle review.

## Exit Planning

Critical vendors must have an exit plan that identifies alternative providers, data return or deletion steps, contract termination rights, and operational transition owners.
