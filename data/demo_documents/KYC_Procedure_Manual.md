# KYC Procedure Manual

Company: HarbourBridge Digital Finance Limited
Owner: Financial Crime Compliance
Version: 2026.1

## Objective

This manual explains how onboarding analysts collect, verify, and approve Know Your Customer information for retail, sole proprietor, and corporate customers. It is synthetic demo content and does not describe any real customer.

## Standard Onboarding Workflow

1. Create an onboarding case in the customer lifecycle system.
2. Collect required identity, address, business, ownership, and authorization documents.
3. Verify document authenticity and expiry dates.
4. Complete sanctions, PEP, and adverse media screening.
5. Assign a risk rating using geography, product, industry, transaction profile, and ownership factors.
6. Request additional source of funds or source of wealth information when risk indicators are present.
7. Submit the case for second-line review if the customer is medium or high risk.
8. Activate the account only after all mandatory checks and approvals are complete.

## Required Documents

Retail customers require:

- Government-issued identity document.
- Proof of address dated within the last three months.
- Mobile number and email verification.
- Source of funds declaration for high-value expected activity.

Corporate customers require:

- Certificate of incorporation or business registration certificate.
- Registered address proof.
- Board resolution or authorization letter naming account operators.
- Identity documents for beneficial owners with 25 percent or more ownership.
- Identity documents for authorized signers.
- Ownership and control structure chart.
- Source of funds or source of wealth declaration when the risk rating is high.

## Enhanced Due Diligence

Enhanced due diligence is required when a customer is high risk, has complex ownership, uses nominee shareholders, operates in high-risk industries, has adverse media involving fraud or money laundering, or has unexplained source of funds. Enhanced due diligence requires Compliance approval before onboarding can continue.

## Screening Rules

Screening must be completed before account activation and refreshed daily for active customers. Potential matches must not be dismissed by the onboarding analyst alone. A true match or unresolved possible match must be escalated to the Money Laundering Reporting Officer.

## Quality Control

Managers review at least 10 percent of approved onboarding cases each month. Critical defects include missing identity documents, unverified beneficial ownership, incomplete sanctions screening, and activation before required approval.

## Reassessment

Customer risk ratings must be reviewed at least annually for high-risk customers and every three years for standard-risk customers. Trigger events such as ownership changes, unusual transaction behavior, or negative news require immediate reassessment.
