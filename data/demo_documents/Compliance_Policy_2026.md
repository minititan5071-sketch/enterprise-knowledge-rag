# Compliance Policy 2026

Company: HarbourBridge Digital Finance Limited
Region: Hong Kong
Audience: All employees, contractors, and approved vendors
Effective date: 2026-01-01

## Purpose

HarbourBridge Digital Finance Limited is a fictional Hong Kong fintech company that provides digital banking, payment, and wealth workflow tools to corporate clients. This policy defines the minimum compliance controls expected for customer onboarding, regulated product communications, record keeping, suspicious activity escalation, and internal governance.

## Compliance Principles

Employees must follow these principles:

- Act honestly, fairly, and with due skill, care, and diligence.
- Maintain accurate records for customer onboarding, product suitability, complaints, operational incidents, and vendor oversight.
- Escalate suspicious activity, privacy concerns, and conduct risks promptly.
- Avoid making unapproved claims about regulated products or investment outcomes.
- Use only approved systems for customer data, regulated records, and internal approvals.

## Governance Roles

The Compliance Committee owns the annual compliance plan and meets monthly. The Money Laundering Reporting Officer reviews suspicious activity escalations. The Data Protection Lead reviews personal data incidents and cross-border transfer questions. The Head of Operations owns remediation tracking for control gaps.

## Customer Onboarding Compliance

Before a customer account may be activated, the onboarding team must confirm that required KYC documents have been collected and reviewed. Required documents include:

- A valid identity document for each beneficial owner and authorized signer.
- Proof of residential address or registered business address dated within the last three months.
- Business registration certificate for corporate customers.
- Ownership and control structure chart for corporate customers.
- Source of funds or source of wealth declaration when risk scoring requires it.
- Sanctions, politically exposed person, and adverse media screening results.

High-risk customers require Compliance approval before activation. Examples include complex ownership structures, sanctions exposure, unexplained source of funds, adverse media involving financial crime, or customers operating in high-risk industries.

## Record Retention

Compliance records must be retained for at least seven years after the relationship ends unless a longer legal hold applies. Records must remain searchable by customer ID, workspace, document type, reviewer, and approval date.

## Marketing and Product Communications

All public-facing marketing materials, product FAQs, and onboarding scripts must be reviewed by Compliance before publication. Employees must not promise guaranteed returns, imply regulatory endorsement, or omit material risk disclosures.

## Breach Escalation

Potential regulatory breaches must be reported to Compliance within one business day. Urgent issues involving suspected data leakage, sanctions exposure, fraud, or customer harm must be escalated immediately through the incident response channel.

## Monitoring

Compliance performs monthly sample reviews of customer onboarding files, quarterly thematic reviews of complaint handling, and annual reviews of employee training completion. Findings are tracked until remediation evidence is accepted.
